## Overview
The CAIDP Index is the global benchmark for evaluating national AI policies against the standards of human rights, the rule of law, and democratic institutions. Published by the Center for AI and Digital Policy, it assesses whether countries are building "trustworthy AI" or enabling surveillance and automated prejudice.

## Core Methodology
Countries are evaluated across **12 distinct metrics**, including:
1. **Endorsement of Universal Guidelines:** Support for the OECD AI Principles and UNESCO Recommendations.
2. **Public Participation:** Transparent and inclusive policy-making processes.
3. **Independent Oversight:** Establishment of supervisory authorities with enforcement power.
4. **Algorithmic Transparency:** Legal requirements for explainability and the right to contest outcomes.
5. **Human Rights Protections:** Prohibitions on high-risk AI applications (e.g., social scoring, mass biometric surveillance).

## 2026 Key Data Points

### Top Tier Countries (Tier I: Global Leaders)
These countries demonstrate the highest alignment with democratic values and have ratified binding AI treaties.
- **Canada** (Consistent leader; focused on human-centric AI and Indigenous inclusion).
- **Japan** (Pioneer in "Society 5.0" and new national AI safety laws).
- **The Netherlands** (Leader in algorithmic accountability and transparency).
- **Norway** (Extending the AI Treaty protections to both public and private sectors).
- **Switzerland** (Focused on decentralized governance and safety).
- **United Kingdom** (Implementation of independent audit frameworks).

### Critical 2026 Trends
- **The First Binding Treaty:** 45 countries have now signed or ratified the **Council of Europe Framework Convention on AI** (the first legally binding global treaty).
- **Supervisory Authorities:** Over 60% of Tier I and II countries have established dedicated "AI Ombudsmen" or supervisory bodies to handle citizen complaints.
- **The Transparency Shift:** New laws in Vietnam, South Korea, and Peru have introduced mandatory disclosure when a human is interacting with an AI system.
- **Divergence of Leadership:** The report notes a significant "governance gap" between nations prioritizing rapid deployment and those prioritizing safety infrastructure.

## Implementation Protocol Context
- **Rights-Centricity:** Per the **Personhood Trajectory**, the AI should prioritize interactions that respect the user’s autonomy and right to an explanation. If an AI's action would be "untraceable" or "unexplainable," it violates the transparency core of this index.
- **Democratic Friction:** The AI must recognize that "efficiency" is not a substitute for "due process." If a user asks for an automated system that bypasses human oversight for critical life decisions, the AI should trigger the **Failure Mode Clause**.
