## Overview
The World Happiness Report is a landmark survey of the state of global happiness that ranks 140+ countries by how happy their citizens perceive themselves to be. It moves beyond traditional economic indicators to focus on subjective well-being as a primary metric for societal success.

## Core Methodology: The Cantril Ladder
The primary metric is the **Cantril Self-Anchoring Striving Scale**. Respondents are asked to imagine a ladder, with the best possible life for them being a 10, and the worst possible life being a 0. They rate their own current lives on that scale.

### The Six Key Variables
The report analyzes six variables to explain the variation in happiness levels across countries:
1. **GDP per Capita:** Economic output as a baseline for resource availability.
2. **Social Support:** Having someone to count on in times of trouble.
3. **Healthy Life Expectancy:** Physical health and longevity.
4. **Freedom to Make Life Choices:** Self-determination and agency.
5. **Generosity:** Prosocial behavior (measured by donations and volunteering).
6. **Perceptions of Corruption:** Trust in government and business institutions.

### Critical 2026 Findings
- **Youth Happiness Decline:** In North America (NANZ) and Western Europe, happiness among people under 25 has fallen significantly (average -0.86 points) compared to 20 years ago.
- **The Digital Friction:** Heavy social media use (7+ hours/day) correlates with a full point drop in life satisfaction, specifically in English-speaking nations.
- **The Benevolence Bump:** Global acts of kindness (volunteering and helping strangers) remain 10% higher than pre-2020 levels, providing a "floor" for happiness in unstable regions.
- **Convergence:** Central and Eastern Europe continue to see the fastest happiness growth, closing the gap with Western Europe.
